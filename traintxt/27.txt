Mechanism of Ginsenoside Rb1 Intestinal Bacterial Transformation in Treatment of Gastric Cancer Based on Network Phar-
macology
Wan Yan， Ao Hui▲
（ Chengdu University of Traditional Chinese Medicine， Chengdu， 611137， China）
【 Abstract】 Objective： Study the mechanism of ginsenoside Rb1 and ginsenoside F2 in the treatment of gastric cancer based on network
pharmacology， and to provide scientific basis for rational clinical application of ginsenoside Rd and ginsenoside F2. Methods： The targets
of ginsenoside Rd and ginsenoside F2 were predicted by SwissTargetPrediction database， and the targets of gastric cancer were obtained
from Disgenet， Malacards and Omim databases with the key word “ Gastriccancer” . The PPI networks of ginsenoside Rd and ginsenoside
F2 were intersected with the PPI network of gastric cancer to obtain the PPI networks of ginsenoside Rd and ginsenoside F2 in the treat-
ment of gastric cancer， respectively. The core targets of ginsenoside Rd and ginsenoside F2 in the treatment of gastric cancer were intro-
duced into Metascape database， and the results of GO analysis and KEGG enrichment were obtained. Results： The results of network
pharmacology showed that there were 64 and 56 key targets of ginsenoside Rd and ginsenoside F2 in the treatment of gastric cancer， re-
spectively. Combined with the biological characteristics of ginsenoside Rd and ginsenoside F2， it was found that PI3K － Akt signal path-
way， MAPK signal pathway， HIF － 1t signal pathway and ErbB signal pathway may be the main pathways involved in the anti － gastric
cancer effects of the two ginsenosides above. Conclusion： Ginsenoside Rb1 intestinal flora transformants － ginsenoside Rd and ginsen-
oside F2 may treat gastric cancer by regulating PI3K － Akt signal pathway， MAPK signal pathway， HIF － 1t signal pathway and ErbB sig-
nal pathway， which can be verified by related experiments.
【 Keywords】 PI3K － Akt signaling pathway； MAPK signaling pathway； HIF － 1t signaling pathway； ErbB signaling pathway； Ginsenoside
Rb1 intestinal flora transformant； Ginsenoside Rd； Ginsenoside F2； Gastric cancer； Network pharmacology
